<p><p>The Peripheral Arterial Disease market features three primary balloon types used in angioplasty procedures. Ordinary balloons are standard, non-coated devices that expand to open narrowed arteries. Drug-eluting balloons are specialized with medication designed to prevent restenosis, releasing drugs that inhibit cell growth. Special balloons include innovative designs like scoring or cutting balloons, which enhance plaque disruption. Each type addresses unique patient needs and conditions, contributing to improved blood flow and reduced symptoms in those suffering from peripheral arterial disease.</p></p>
