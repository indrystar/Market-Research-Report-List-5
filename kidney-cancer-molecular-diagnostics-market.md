<p><p>The kidney cancer molecular diagnostics market encompasses various techniques crucial for detecting genetic alterations associated with the disease. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies specific DNA sequences, enhancing sensitivity for identifying mutations. FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization) visualizes chromosomal abnormalities, aiding in diagnosis and prognosis. DNA sequencing provides comprehensive insights into genetic variations at the nucleotide level, facilitating personalized treatment approaches. Gene chips, or microarrays, analyze multiple genes simultaneously, enabling the assessment of gene expression profiles related to kidney cancer.</p></p>
