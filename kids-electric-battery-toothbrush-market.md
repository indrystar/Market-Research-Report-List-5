In contrast, rotation-oscillation toothbrushes feature round brush heads that rotate in one direction and then the opposite, effectively removing plaque through a combination of circular motion and back-and-forth action. Both types aim to promote better oral hygiene habits among children, making brushing fun and efficient.</p></p>
