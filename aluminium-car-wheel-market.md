<p><p>The aluminum car wheel market can be categorized into three primary types: casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create intricate designs, offering flexibility in shapes and sizes. Forging, on the other hand, entails shaping solid aluminum under high pressure, resulting in wheels that are stronger and lighter. Other methods may include extrusion and machining processes, which can also produce specialized wheel designs. Each type serves distinct purposes based on performance, aesthetics, and manufacturing efficiencies.</p></p>
