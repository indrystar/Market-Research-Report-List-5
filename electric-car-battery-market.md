<p><p>The electric car battery market primarily consists of three types: lithium-ion batteries, nickel-metal hydride (Ni-MH) batteries, and fuel cells. Lithium-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density and efficiency, making them the preferred choice for most electric vehicles. Ni-MH batteries, while less common now, are known for their durability and were previously used in hybrid vehicles. Fuel cells convert hydrogen into electricity, offering fast refueling and longer ranges, positioning them as an alternative for future electric mobility solutions.</p></p>
