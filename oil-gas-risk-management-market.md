<p><p>Oil and gas risk management consists of two primary market types: initial risk management and residual risk management. Initial risk management focuses on identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks during the exploration and production phases, aiming to minimize potential losses from uncertainties. In contrast, residual risk management deals with risks that remain post-mitigation, ensuring that companies have strategies in place to handle unforeseen events or market fluctuations, thereby maintaining operational stability and financial integrity throughout the value chain.</p></p>
