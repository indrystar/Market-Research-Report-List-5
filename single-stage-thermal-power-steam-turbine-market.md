<p><p>The single-stage thermal power steam turbine market encompasses various types, including condensing and back pressure turbines, among others. Condensing turbines are designed to convert steam into water by utilizing a condenser, optimizing efficiency for electricity generation. Back pressure turbines, on the other hand, extract energy while maintaining steam pressure, often used in cogeneration systems for combined heat and power applications. Other types may include extraction turbines or small-scale turbines, catering to specific operational needs and energy demands within the market.</p></p>

Closed systems recycle steam, maximizing efficiency by reusing condensate, making them ideal for continuous power generation and reducing water consumption. Both configurations serve specific energy needs, balancing efficiency and operational simplicity for varying industrial applications.</p></p>
