<p><ul><li>CCD</li><li>CMOS</li><li>EMCCD</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Scientific research cameras primarily consist of three types: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device), CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor), and EMCCD (Electron Multiplying Charge-Coupled Device). CCD cameras provide high-quality images with low noise, making them ideal for sensitive applications. CMOS cameras are known for their faster readout speeds and lower power consumption, suitable for various imaging tasks. EMCCD cameras enhance sensitivity by amplifying signals, making them perfect for low-light conditions. Each type serves distinct research needs across fields like biology, astronomy, and materials science.</p></p>
