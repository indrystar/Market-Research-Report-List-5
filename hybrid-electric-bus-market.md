<p><p>The Hybrid Electric Bus market is categorized into Type I and Type II based on powertrain configurations. Type I buses typically use a parallel hybrid system, combining an internal combustion engine with an electric motor, allowing both to power the vehicle independently or together. Type II buses employ a series hybrid system, where the engine generates electricity to power the electric motor, which drives the wheels. Both types aim to reduce emissions and improve fuel efficiency in urban transit systems.</p></p>
